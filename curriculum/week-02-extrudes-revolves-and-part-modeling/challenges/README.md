# Week 2 — Challenges

The exercises drill the basics. **Challenges stretch you.** This one takes 90–150 minutes and produces a portfolio-worthy part: a real object you reverse-engineer from a photo and a few measurements, then model robustly enough to survive edits.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Shelled bottle or cup from a photo](challenge-01-shelled-bottle.md)** — revolve a real bottle/cup profile, shell it to a uniform wall, add a filleted base, and reproduce a target part from an image plus key dimensions. (~120 min)

## How challenges are assessed

Challenges are **optional** — you can pass the week without them. But doing this one puts you measurably ahead, because it forces every Week 2 skill into one part *and* adds the thing exercises can't: **judgement under ambiguity.** A guided exercise hands you every dimension. A photo hands you a shape and makes you decide what the dimensions are.

Assessment is by self-review against the rubric in the challenge file, plus optional peer review via your shared OnShape link. The four things that distinguish a great submission from a passing one:

1. **Fidelity** — does the model actually look like the target object, in proportion and detail?
2. **Uniform wall** — is the shell genuinely uniform (verified in a section view), with no thin spots or breakthroughs?
3. **Robustness** — does it pass the rebuild test? Change the bottle height by 20% and the wall thickness by 1 mm; nothing red, wall still uniform, base still filleted.
4. **Feature-list quality** — short, ordered, every feature renamed, the fillet-vs-shell ordering decision made correctly and explained.

The skills this challenge builds — revolve a real profile, shell uniformly, reason about feature order under a thin wall — reappear directly in the **mini-project** (your finished part) and again in **Week 6's capstone** (a multi-part mechanism where at least one part is a shelled, revolved body). Reverse-engineering from a photo is also exactly how you'll work in industry when someone hands you a competitor's part and says "make ours like this, but better."
