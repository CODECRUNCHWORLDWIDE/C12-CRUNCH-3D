# Week 5 — Challenges

The exercises drill each skill in isolation. **The challenge stretches you** by combining them into one manufacturing-grade deliverable. It takes 90–150 minutes and produces a drawing you can put in a portfolio — the kind of sheet a real machine shop would accept "made to print."

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — A manufacturing-ready, configured drawing](./challenge-01-manufacturing-ready-drawing.md)** — take an earlier part, fully dimension and tolerance it (section view + GD&T frame), configure it into metric size variants, and produce a sheet that switches between configurations from a dropdown. (~120 min)

## How it's assessed

The challenge is optional — you can pass the week without it. If you do it, you'll be measurably ahead, because it forces the two halves of the week (documentation + parameterization) to coexist on one sheet, which is exactly what a working CAD job looks like.

Assessment is by a **rubric** (in the challenge file) across five dimensions: drawing completeness, tolerancing correctness, GD&T validity, configuration robustness, and manufacturability/readability. "Great" means a competent machinist could make any size variant from your PDF without emailing you a single question. Submit a **public OnShape link** plus the exported **PDF** so a peer (or instructor) can both inspect the live model and read the flat sheet.
